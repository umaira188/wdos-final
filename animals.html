INDIGENOUS ANMALS IN SRI LANKA

"Indigenous Animals"

Sri Lanka, the pearl of the Indian Ocean, is a land teeming with unique and fascinating creatures. Its geographical isolation and diverse habitats have fostered an extraordinary level of endemism, meaning a staggering number of species found nowhere else on Earth. The unique ecosystems found in Sri Lanka, including rainforests, dry forests, coastal areas, and wetlands, contribute to the presence of a variety of wildlife species.Here's a brief introduction to some of the indigenous animals in Sri Lanka:


Ceylon Birdwing
(Troides darsius)

Sri Lanka's largest species of butterflies. Found in tropical rainforests, this species is distinguished by its striking black and green wings.

Indian Pangolin
(Manis crassicaudata)

An unusual-looking species of scaly anteater. Mostly nocturnal, it uses its long, sticky tongue to eat termites and ants.

Purple-faced Langur
(Semnopithecus vetulus)

A species of endemic primate distinguished by its purple face. Lives in Sri Lanka's rainforests and is frequently observed in groups.

Sri Lanka Junglefowl
(Gallus lafayettii)

The Sri Lankan national bird. Famous for having feathers that are vivid and colorful, along with wattles and a unique comb.

Loris
(Nycticebus spp.)

Primate nocturnal species characterized by big eyes and slow, deliberate movements. Found in a variety of woodland environments and distinguished by their distinct way of moving.

Toque Macaque
(Macaca sinica)

A monkey of medium size with a distinctive hair "toque" on its head. Found in a range of environments, such as cities, woods, and cultural sites.

Sri Lanka Frogmouth
(Batrachostomus moniliger)

An interesting bird species native to Sri Lanka, known for its cryptic plumage and nocturnal habits.

Indian Star Tortoise
(Geochelone elegans)

Found in certain regions of Sri Lanka, the Indian star tortoise is known for its attractive shell pattern.

Kandyan Dwarf Toad
(Adenomus kandianus)

This toad is endemic to Sri Lanka and is primarily found in the central hills region.
